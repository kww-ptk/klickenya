Days 1–2: Nairobi

Most first-timers treat Nairobi as a transit city. That's a mistake. Nairobi is one of Africa's most dynamic capitals — a city of world-class restaurants, vibrant art galleries, and an outdoor scene that will surprise you. Give it two full days before heading into the wild.

Left: Nairobi CBD from Westlands. Right: the city's legendary food and market culture.

02

📍 Nairobi

Giraffe Centre · Karen Blixen · Kibera arts

Wildlife · History · Culture · Photography

8:30 AM

Giraffe Centre

Feed Rothschild giraffes at eye level from the raised platform. One of the best wildlife encounters you can have within 20 minutes of a capital city.

🦒 Highlight
11:00 AM

Karen Blixen Museum

The original Out of Africa farmhouse, perfectly preserved. The views of the Ngong Hills haven't changed since 1931.

1:30 PM

Lunch at Talisman, Karen

Garden restaurant in a colonial-era house. The pan-fried tilapia and passion fruit cheesecake are non-negotiable.

4:00 PM

Kibera arts walk

One of Africa's most vibrant creative scenes. Visit the Maasai Mbili gallery and buy directly from artists at fair prices.

🎨 Unmissable
🎫 Entries: KSh 2,200 🍽 Food: KSh 2,800 🚕 Transport: KSh 1,200
💡

Pro tip — Getting around Nairobi

Download the Little (Uber for Kenya) app before you land. Always negotiate matatu fares before boarding. Bolt is also reliable. Avoid hailing unmarked taxis at night — always use apps.

Days 3–5: Maasai Mara

If there's one reason to come to Kenya, this is it. The Maasai Mara National Reserve is one of the last great wildlife spectacles on earth. Three days gives you enough time to experience it properly — including a sunrise drive on day four that will rearrange your understanding of what's possible.

"The moment you see a lion pride in the golden grass — you understand, on a cellular level, why humans came from this place."

— James Kariuki, after his third Mara visit

📍 Nairobi → Maasai Mara

Transfer day · Afternoon game drive

3.5hr drive · Sunset through the Mara

6:00 AM

Early morning departure

Leave Nairobi before sunrise to avoid traffic. Stop at Narok town for breakfast — the chapati-and-egg stalls are exceptional.

9:30 AM

Enter the Mara

Gate briefing, vehicle check. Your guide will immediately begin tracking — the animals don't wait for introductions.

4:00 PM

Sunset game drive

The best light of the day. Position yourself near the Mara River for hippo activity and potential leopard sightings in the riverine forest.

🌅 Golden hour
🚗 Drive or fly: KSh 2,000–18,000🏕 Camp: KSh 8,000
🚁

Upgrade worth considering

The hot air balloon safari at dawn costs around KSh 30,000 per person — but floating over 500 wildebeest as the sun rises is one of the most extraordinary things a human can experience. Book directly through your camp, 2–3 days in advance.

Days 6–8: The Coast

After the intensity of the Mara, the Kenya coast delivers a completely different magic: coral-blue water, 14th-century Swahili architecture, and a languid, salt-kissed pace of life. We recommend splitting these three days between Diani Beach and a quick trip to Lamu — one of Africa's most beautifully preserved medieval towns.

Left: Diani Beach, Kwale County. Right: Lamu Old Town — UNESCO World Heritage Site, founded in the 14th century.

⚠️

Don't make this mistake

Don't fly directly from Nairobi to the coast without booking accommodation in advance — especially if you're visiting during December–January high season. Diani fills up completely. Book at least 3 weeks ahead on Klickenya.
